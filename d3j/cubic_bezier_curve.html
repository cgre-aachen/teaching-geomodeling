Cubic Bézier Curve - Theory

A cubic Bézier curve is defined by four control points. The first and last points are the endpoints of the curve, and the intermediate two points determine the shape of the curve. The curve is parameterized by a parameter \( t \) which runs from 0 to 1.

The mathematical representation of a cubic Bézier curve is:

\( B(t) = (1-t)^3P_0 + 3(1-t)^2tP_1 + 3(1-t)t^2P_2 + t^3P_3 \)

where \( P_0, P_1, P_2, \) and \( P_3 \) are the control points and \( t \) varies from 0 to 1.

Bézier curves are widely used in computer graphics to model smooth curves that can be scaled indefinitely.
