Polynomial Regression - Theory

Polynomial regression is a form of regression analysis in which the relationship between the independent variable x and the dependent variable y is modeled as an nth degree polynomial.

The general form of a polynomial of degree n is:

\( y = a_0 + a_1x + a_2x^2 + \ldots + a_nx^n \)

Polynomial regression fits a nonlinear relationship between the value of x and the corresponding conditional mean of y, denoted \( E(y | x) \). Although polynomial regression fits a nonlinear model to the data, as a statistical estimation problem, it is linear in terms of the unknown parameters \( a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_n \).
